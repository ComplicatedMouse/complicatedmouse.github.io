Random Emission Position

This section describes the Random Emission Position feature for emitting particles from random locations.

Overview

The Random Emission Position feature emits particles from random locations inside a virtual spherical body.
When an emitter shape is divided into equal parts, those parts can serve as the central positions of randomly located particle emissions.
The following overview describes the settings ().

Random Emission Position

This item is shared by all emitter shapes. Specifies the radius of a sphere with the emission position as the center.

Example Configurations

The following example settings and image show what happens when the emitter shape is Circle (Evenly Divided) and the number of divisions is set to 4.

A
Equally divided locations A circle emitter shape divided into four even portions.
B Virtual Sphere A virtual sphere, with the position of particle emission at its center.
C Location of Particle Generation

Particles are generated at random locations inside the emission range defined by the virtual sphere B.
D Direction of particle movement Particles move in the direction of emission from the emitter.
Particles generated from the same virtual sphere move in the same direction.
