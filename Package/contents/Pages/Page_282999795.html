Capture Pane

This section describes the capture pane, which captures the results of frame buffer and layout rendering and converts the results to an image.

Overview

The capture pane can capture the results of rendering the framebuffer or the layout and use them as textures.
Pane rendering is not normally performed for panes lower the capture pane () hierarchy in the Hierarchy window.

The texture image created by the capture pane is displayed in the Texture window, delineated from the normal texture by a yellow stripe (█).
As with a normal texture, this texture can be used after being registered in Texture () in the property window.
The following are the main settings that can be configured in the capture pane.

  • The area to be captured as the texture
  • Whether to capture the framebuffer or only the initial frame
  • Clear color to specify the color value or alpha value of the knockout area

Limitations

Following are capture pane restrictions.

  • Restrictions on parent/child relationships
    • You cannot create parent/child relationship structures that cause dependencies to occur in the capture texture.
      Examples include structures that place picture panes in the children of the capture pane that contain textures created by the capture pane.
  • Restrictions on rotation and scaling
    • If rotation or scaling are configured for the capture texture, you cannot perform capture following the shape of the pane.
  • The Layout window does not support capture effect rendering.
    Because of this limitation, the thumbnails of capture effects do not display correctly.

  • The capture texture result varies because blending is applied twice: once by the blending process of the capture effect Rendering Mode, and once by the blending process of the capture pane.
