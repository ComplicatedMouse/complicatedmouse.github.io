Near Alpha and Camera Distance Scale Restriction (Near)

This section describes how to use the Near Alpha and Camera Distance Scale Restriction (Near) features to reduce the rendering load that arises when particles are drawn with a large surface area that covers the camera.
The following images compare the differences between when the Near Alpha and Camera Distance Scale Restriction (Far) check boxes () are selected and when they are cleared.

ON The Camera Distance Scale Restriction (Near) feature controls the display size of the particle as it approaches the camera.
The Near Alpha feature alters the opacity of the particle so it appears to naturally fade and disappear as it approaches the camera.
OFF The particle is covered by the camera when the camera nears, resulting in a large rendering load.

Related Links For more information, see the following pages.

Near Alpha This feature applies translucency to particles when they are in near distance of the camera.

This feature limits scaling so particles in front of the camera are no larger than necessary.
