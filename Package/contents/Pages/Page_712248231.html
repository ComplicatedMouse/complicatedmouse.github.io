Automatically Detect Problems

After you take a profile, go to the Checkers tab for a list of automatically detected problems in your game. There are 50 different checkers that examine the recorded profile as well as perform static code analysis on your game code.



Issues are listed from most severe to least severe:

 Fix now: Critical problems with the recording of the profile.

 High risk or high priority performance issues.

 Medium priority performance issues.

 Low priority performance or code quality issues.

 Informational purposes only.


At the bottom of the window, you can filter the list to only look at particular issues, such as Performance.


To examine a Checker, click anywhere on the title bar to expand it.

For Best Results

Checkers work best when there is enough detail per frame, so use a sample rate of at least 100x per frame (the default). Also ensure you are taking a Callstack profile (the default).

Some checkers only trigger if the profile recorded additional data such as Performance Counters,  Threads tracing, or  Graphics tracing (enabled in the Sampled Profile tab).  Full Integration Setup is required to enable either  Threads tracing or  Graphics tracing.

Disabling Checkers if they aren't Helpful

If you've determined that a Checker is unhelpful, click the  button inside of the Checker and it will stop showing up in the results.

To enable it again, click on the Manager Checkers button at the top, find the checker, expand the checker, and click on the  button.
