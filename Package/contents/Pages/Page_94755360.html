Billboard and Y-Axis Billboard

This section describes the differences between Billboard and Y-Axis Billboard.

Overview

Billboard is a type of particle where the position of the camera affects the orientation of the particle. These particles come in two types: Billboard particles and Y-Axis Billboard particles.
The figure shows how they differ when the camera moves.

A Billboard

This type of particle always faces in the direction of the camera by rotating around all axes.

Mapping a circular texture on a slab polygon creates the appearance of a sphere.
B Y-axial billboard

This type of particle always faces in the direction of the camera but only rotates around all the y-axis.

Particles elongated in the direction of the y-axis appear as cylinders. This behavior is useful for elongated effects like laser beams.
