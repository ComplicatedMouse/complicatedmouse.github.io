Soft Particles

This section describes the Soft Particles feature that applies translucency to the borders of particles that overlap with models.

Overview

The Soft Particles feature applies translucency to the borders of particles when models and particles overlap.
As a result, border lines fade and the effect becomes more natural.

The following images show the alpha changes when the █ particle surface and the █ floor surface overlap from the viewpoint.

A The alpha of locations where particles and the floor surface overlap or where the particles are masked becomes 0.
B The alpha for the outermost vertices and fragments becomes 1.

This functionality is set in Distance. You can set the range for applying the alpha.

The following overview describes the settings ().

Soft Particles

Select the processing method to use for soft particles from among the following presets.

None Soft particles are not used.
Processing by Vertex

When a particle overlaps a model, translucency is applied to keep the edges from standing out, considering the thickness of the particle vertices.
Processing takes place in the vertex shader.
Processing by Fragment

When a particle overlaps a model, translucency is applied to particle fragments to keep the edges from standing out.

The fragment shader handles this effect on a per-pixel basis.
Masking Check

Allows you to make particles transparent when they overlap a model by varying their overall alpha value.

You can widen the range for determining the overlap with the masking object by increasing the Sampling Radius.
