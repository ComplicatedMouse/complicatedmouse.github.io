Important Information About Using the Microphone Feature

Overview

This page provides a summary of important information about development of NX applications that use the microphone feature.

Noise is occasionally mixed in with the input of USB microphones and headsets connected to the USB port of the SDEV cradle or Nintendo Switch dock.

It has been confirmed that for some makes of USB microphones and headsets that can attach to the USB port of the SDEV cradle or the Nintendo Switch dock, noise is occasionally mixed in with the microphone input. This noise is due to interference from a TV or the AC adapter connected to the cradle or the dock.

This behavior is by design. There is no workaround.

The extent and characteristics of the noise will vary, depending on the make of the USB microphones and headsets.

Noise is sometimes present in input from microphones connected to the audio jack.

For some headsets and headphone microphone products that can be connected to the audio jack, noise will be mixed into the microphone input when used on the NX retail device, SDEV, or EDEV while the AC adapter is connected.

This behavior is by design. There is no workaround.

The magnitude and characteristics of the noise differ according to the specific headset or headphone microphone product.

Notes About the Characteristics of the Microphone

The characteristics of the microphone differ depending on the model. Additionally, there may be variations among microphones of the same model. The following examples show how they can differ.

  • Microphones of different models can differ greatly in terms of sensitivity and in terms of volume (both maximum and minimum volume).
  • The sensitivity at 1 kHz of microphones of the same model can differ as much as 2.5 times. At other frequencies, the variation may be as much as 3-fold.

For this reason, your application must not be dependent on the characteristics of any specific microphone.
We recommend the following implementation to ensure that your application is not dependent on the characteristics of any specific microphone.

  • Enable the adjustment of the sensitivity of the microphone.
    If the application uses two microphones, enable the adjustment of each one separately.
    You can choose any method for adjusting the sensitivity. Implement a method such as using the application menu or selecting to adjust the sensitivity in an options menu that is called up during gameplay.


Usable Microphones

The following types of microphones can be used with the NX.

  • USB audio class compliant USB microphones.
    • The only microphones that are confirmed to work with NX are the Wii U microphone, Wii U wireless microphone, and official Nintendo licensed microphones for NX.
  • Headsets and earphones attached to the system's audio jack
    • The only headset microphones confirmed to work with NX are official Nintendo licensed products for NX.

Bluetooth microphones are not supported.

If your game is intentionally designed to support only some types of microphone, we recommend employing some means of informing the user what the application supports.
For example, in scenes where you prompt the user to attach a microphone, display an image showing the kind of microphone that the application supports.
