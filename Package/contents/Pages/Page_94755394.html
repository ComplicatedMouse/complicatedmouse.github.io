Directional Billboard and Directional Polygon

This section describes the differences between Directional Billboard and Directional Polygon.

Overview

For particle types with Directional in their name, the speed of the particle affects the direction of the particle.
There are two kinds of directional particle types: directional billboard and directional polygon.

Differences Between Billboards and Directionals

The following table compares billboard and directional billboard. Speed is applied in all directions to the emitters together.

A Billboard This looks strange for particles with directionality.
B Directional Billboard Particles tend toward the direction of velocity, so this is useful for particles with directionality.
Info

Directional particles that depend on particle speed do not display when the particle speed is zero.

Differences Between Directional Billboard and Directional Polygon

The figure shows how directional billboard and directional polygon differ when the camera moves.

A Directional Billboard

This type of particle rotates around the y-axis, which is in the direction of velocity, while the z-axis always faces in the direction of the camera.

Particles elongated in the direction of the y-axis appear as cylinders. This is useful for effects like fireworks moving along a long, curved path.
B Directional Polygon

This type of particle has its y-axis pointing in the direction of velocity.

Unlike directional billboard, you can use rotation animation around the y-axis with this type of particle. This is useful for effects like leaves fluttering in the air.
