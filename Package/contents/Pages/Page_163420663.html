Wired Connection

To use the development hardware with a wired connection, it must be connected to either the SDEV cradle or the Switch dock, and these must be connected to a USB-Ethernet adapter and powered using the dedicated AC adapter.

An SDEV (or EDEV) 1.8 or later must be used to use a wired connection via the SDEV cradle or Switch dock.

Due to hardware constraints, SDEV versions prior to 1.8, do not support a direct connection with a USB-Ethernet adapter that does not go through the dock.
Operations for other hardware released in the future is also not guaranteed at this time.

You must supply your own USB-Ethernet adapter because one is not bundled with the development hardware.

For more information about USB-Ethernet adapter compatibility with the development hardware, see the section on connecting USB devices in the SDEV Operations Manual and the EDEV Operations Manual.

Only one USB Ethernet adapter can be used at a time.
If multiple USB Ethernet adapters are connected, detach them all and then reconnect just the one you intend to use.

Note

Watch for troubles with the SDEV cradle and the SDEV cradle power adapter. If the USB Ethernet adapter is not recognized, make sure that power is being supplied to the USB port of the SDEV cradle.

Setting Up the USB-Ethernet Adapter (SDEV)

You can use communications features by connecting a USB-Ethernet adapter to the USB port of the SDEV cradle.

Use the following procedure to connect a USB-Ethernet adapter.

  1. Turn off power by unplugging the included Wii U AC adapter from the top of the SDEV.
  2. Remove the AC adapter from the power outlet.
  3. Connect the SDEV system to the SDEV cradle.
  4. Connect a USB-Ethernet adapter to the USB port of the SDEV cradle.
  5. Connect the AC adapter to the SDEV cradle.
  6. Connect the AC adapter to a power outlet.
    • The TV output LED on the SDEV cradle lights up for a few seconds and then turns off.
    • If the SDEV cradle's TV output indicator does not light up, disconnect the AC adapter from the outlet, wait for 10 seconds, and then reconnect it.
  7. Turn power back on to the SDEV by plugging the included Wii U AC adapter into the connector on the top of the SDEV system.

Warning

Always use the SDEV cradle that came with the system to avoid malfunction. Also, using the SDEV cradle is not supported by SDEV 1.6.
