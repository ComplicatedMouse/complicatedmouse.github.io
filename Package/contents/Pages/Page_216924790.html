Playing Sound in SoundMaker and AtkPlayer

1. Overview

This page describes how to play sounds in SoundMaker and AtkPlayer.

SoundMaker plays sounds on a PC and is only available in a PC version.
AtkPlayer is available in a PC version and hardware version. The PC version plays sounds using the keyboard for operations, and the hardware version plays sounds using the controller for operations.

Info

See Playing Multiple Sounds in SoundMaker and AtkPlayer when playing back multiple sounds.

2. Playing and Stopping Sounds in SoundMaker

2.1 Playing a Sound in SoundMaker

1. In the Sound Set tab, in the list region, select the item you want to play.

2. At the top of the main window or in the list region, click the Play button to play the sound in the PC speakers.

2.2. Stopping the Currently Playing Sound in SoundMaker

At the top of the main window, click the Stop All button to stop the currently playing sound.

3. Playing and Stopping Sounds in AtkPlayer

3.1. Playing Sounds in AtkPlayer

3.1.1. Playing Sounds From AtkPlayer

1. Open AtkPlayer.

2. Display the name of the sound you want to play in the SoundArchivePlayer panel. Press the RIGHT ARROW and LEFT ARROW keys to change which sound to play.

3. Press the A Button (or A key) to play the sound.

3.1.2. Playing Sounds From SoundMaker

1. Open AtkPlayer.

2. Connect AtkPlayer and SoundMaker.

3. In the Sound Set tab, in the list region, select the item you want to play.

4. At the top of the main window or in the list region, click the Play button to play the sound in AtkPlayer.

3.2. Stopping the Currently Playing Sound in AtkPlayer

3.2.1. Stopping a Sound From AtkPlayer

While in the SoundArchivePlayer panel, press the B Button (or B key) to stop the currently playing sound.

3.2.2. Stopping a Sound From SoundMaker

SoundMaker must be connected to AtkPlayer for this to work.

At the top of the main window, click the Stop All button to stop the currently playing sound.

4. Troubleshooting

SoundMaker

The currently playing sound is not audible.

  • Confirm that headphones or speakers are connected to the PC.
  • In the Tools > Options > Audio tab, check whether the correct device is specified as the sound output device.

The Stop All button does not stop the currently playing sound.

  • Use the Stop All Channels option in the toolbar to stop the currently playing sound.
    This can potentially happen in sound regions in the currently playing sound in which percussion mode is enabled for instruments for which the Env R parameter is set to 0 or a value close to 0.

AtkPlayer

The currently playing sound does not stop.

  • Restart AtkPlayer.
    This can potentially happen if the currently playing sound has the Env R parameter set to 0 or a value close to 0. In particular, when playing a looped waveform for which the Env R parameter is set to 0, the sound cannot be stopped using the normal stop operation.

In the SoundArchivePlayer panel, no instruments are found even after changing the sound name.

    • AtkPlayer does not support playback of sounds on a per-instrument basis.
