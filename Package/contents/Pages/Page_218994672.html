Playing Multiple Sounds in SoundMaker and AtkPlayer

1. Overview

This page describes how to play multiple sounds at the same time, which can be useful when checking how sound effects sound over background music, for example.

In both SoundMaker and AtkPlayer, up to four different sounds can be played at the same time.

Note

This simultaneous playback refers to the ability to playback in parallel, and not to the ability to begin simultaneous audio.

2. Playing Multiple Sounds

2.1. Playing Multiple Sounds in SoundMaker

1. Add sounds to the Preview Player. Use either of the following methods to add the sounds.

  • In the Sound Set tab, in the list region, select and then play the sounds.
  • Drag the sound index cells from the list region in the Sound Set tab to the Preview Player.

2. Also add sounds to the empty rows in Preview Player.

3. To play the desired sounds at the same time, in Preview Player, click the Play buttons next to the rows to which the sounds from step 1 have been added.


Info

Sounds can also be played by selecting a row that a sound has been added to and then clicking the Play button in the toolbar or using the shortcut key.


2.2. Playing Multiple Sounds in AtkPlayer

2.2.1. Playing Sounds From AtkPlayer

1. Open AtkPlayer.

2. In SoundArchivePlayer, use the ← and → buttons to select a sound, and then press the A Button (or A key) to play the sound.

3. Use the ↑ and ↓ buttons to change the handles.

4. Use the ← and → buttons to select another sound, and then press the A Button (or A key) to play that sound while the sound from step 1 continues to play.

2.2.2. Playing Sounds From SoundMaker

1. Open AtkPlayer.

2. Connect AtkPlayer and SoundMaker.

3. Any operations performed in the Preview Player panel of SoundMaker are applied to the SoundArchivePlayer panel of AtkPlayer. For more information about using Preview Player, see Playing Multiple Sounds in SoundMaker.

3. Troubleshooting

Common

Previously played sounds stop playing, or subsequently played sounds cannot be played.

  • After the number of currently playing sounds exceeds the maximum, currently playing sounds may be stopped (according to sound priority), or you may be unable to play new sounds. Check the player sound limit or the number of sounds setting in the sound archive in Project > Project Settings > Sound Archive Settings Tab 1.
    • 　
  • If this happens when playing multiple sounds in AtkPlayer, check whether the single playback setting is enabled for the target sound in SoundMaker. If the single playback setting is set to a value other than Do not use, you may be unable to play that sound at the same time as other sounds.
