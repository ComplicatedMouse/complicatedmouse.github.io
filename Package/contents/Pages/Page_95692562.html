Billboard and Plane Polygon

This section describes the differences between Billboard and Plane Polygon.

Overview

Plane Polygon particles are not dependent on the position of the camera. These particles come in two types: XY Plane Polygon and XZ Plane Polygon.
Billboard is a type of particle where the position of the camera affects the orientation of the particle. These particles come in two types: Billboard particles and Y-Axis Billboard particles.
The following animation demonstrates the difference between Billboard particles and Plane Polygon particles. Notice what happens to the two polygons mapped with circular textures when the camera moves.

A Billboard

This type of particle always faces in the direction of the camera by rotating around all axes.

Mapping a circular texture on a slab polygon creates the appearance of a sphere. This behavior works for effects such as balls and snow.
B XY-plane polygon The particle is positioned so that its XY plane matches the XY plane in emitter coordinate space.
