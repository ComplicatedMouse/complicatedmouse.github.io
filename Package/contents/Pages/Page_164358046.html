Add-on Sound Archives

Overview

Add-on sound archives are used to add sounds or banks for playback in combination with the sound archive that is normally used.

This feature supports the following types of use cases.

Delivery of Downloadable Content

Add-on sound archives can be created to provide sounds and banks as downloadable content for use in combination with the existing, released sound archive.

You can reuse banks from a sound archive and include just the sequence sound data in the add-on sound archive.

Splitting Up a Sound Archive for Use

Add-on sound archives can be used as a substitute for user-managed groups. Create add-on sound archives for loading as individual game resources, dynamically registering and unregistering them with the sound archive player.

Unlike user-managed groups, add-on sound archives are separate archives from the main sound archive, and that has the following benefits.

  • The archives are small in size and dependency scope, so conversion should take less time.
  • They are highly independent, so conflicts are less of an issue when multiple people are editing the main sound archive and the add-on sound archives at the same time.

Specifications

The add-on sound archive handles items as follows.

Items that can be added to add-on sound archives
  • Wave sounds
  • Stream sounds
  • Sequence sounds
  • Bank
  • Waveform archives
Add-on sound archives
Sound archives
  • Player
  • Bank
Items that cannot be added to add-on sound archives
  • Group
  • Player

Add-on sound archives do not contain players, but use the player in the main sound achive for playback.

The sequence sound for add-on sound archives can use banks from either the main or add-on sound archives.

However, the main sound archive cannot reference items in add-on sound archives.
An add-on sound archive is also unable to reference items from another add-on sound archive.


Info

If items are registered that cannot be used by SoundMaker, there will be an error at conversion time or the nn::atk library will ignore them when they are loaded.
