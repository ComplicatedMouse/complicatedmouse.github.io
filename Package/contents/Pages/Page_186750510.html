Particle

This section describes the particles (polygons) emitted from the emitter.


The particle is emitted with an initial velocity and its behavior is affected by external forces such as fields until it reaches the end of its lifespan and disappears.
With EffectMaker you can collectively control the movements of large volumes of particles.

Particles have the following characteristics.

  • The emitters are rendered as game effects.
  • Emitters are assigned initial values such as initial velocity, lifespan, scale, and color, and then released.
  • The possible particle shapes are the particles or stripe particles generated during the runtime or primitive particles that use 3D models.
  • There are particles that affect the camera position or velocity in addition to the type of particles that are rendered as they are generated.

Stripe Particle

A stripe particle is a string shape formed of linked polygons.
It can be used in cases such as the following.

Tracing the trajectory of moving models such as swords The trajectory of sparks released from an emitter

Stripe Types

The types of stripe particles.
There are consolidated stripes that connect the displayed particles, and historical stripes that connect the history between each particle.
